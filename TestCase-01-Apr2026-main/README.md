# Research Project: Newsletter / Email Marketing for B2B SaaS

This repository contains structured research on practitioners who actively share high-quality, actionable insights about newsletter and email marketing for B2B SaaS.

## Why this topic

Email remains one of the highest-leverage channels for B2B SaaS because it directly supports activation, retention, expansion, and pipeline generation. This research project focuses on collecting real-world frameworks and examples that can later be turned into an execution playbook.

## Research objective

Build a curated library of expert content from active operators across LinkedIn, YouTube, podcasts, and related channels.

## Expert selection criteria

Experts are selected based on:

- Consistent publishing activity in the last 3-6 months
- Clear practitioner evidence (real campaigns, examples, metrics, or breakdowns)
- Strong relevance to B2B SaaS newsletter or lifecycle email strategy
- Cross-channel credibility (LinkedIn, YouTube, podcasts, newsletter, or case studies)

## Repository structure

- `research/sources.md` - master index of experts, links, dates, and annotations
- `research/linkedin-posts/` - collected LinkedIn posts grouped by author
- `research/youtube-transcripts/` - video transcript notes grouped by video
- `research/other/` - podcast notes, blog posts, newsletter issues, and related assets

## Data collection method

### LinkedIn posts

- Captured manually from each expert's public posts
- Each entry includes post URL, publish date, short summary, and key takeaway

### YouTube transcripts

- Collected via API-based method (Supadata or equivalent free transcript workflow)
- Each transcript file includes source URL, date, summary, and practical takeaways

### Other materials

- Includes podcasts, newsletter archives, interviews, and long-form articles
- Notes focus on reusable frameworks for B2B SaaS teams

## What was collected

- `research/sources.md` includes 10 selected experts with profile links, primary channels, collection-window metadata, and short inclusion annotations.
- `research/linkedin-posts/` includes one author file per expert with three source entries and reusable newsletter/email takeaways.
- `research/youtube-transcripts/` includes one transcript note file per expert. Where direct YouTube evidence was not verified, files explicitly document alternative evidence and confidence level.
- `research/other/` includes additional cross-channel references (newsletter pages, podcast links, interviews, and related long-form sources) to support triangulation.

## Why these experts

Experts were selected for practitioner credibility over theory-only commentary. Priority was given to operators with:

- recent publishing consistency,
- clear evidence of execution (examples, systems, outcomes),
- strong relevance to B2B SaaS newsletter and lifecycle email strategy,
- cross-channel presence that allows source verification beyond a single platform.
